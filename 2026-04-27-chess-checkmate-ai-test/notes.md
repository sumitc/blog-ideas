# Notes: I Tested ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini on Chess

## Origin Story
- April 2026: Playing chess with daughter (she's just started learning)
- One game ended in a position that looked like a clear checkmate for White
- Daughter asked to verify on ChatGPT before calling it
- Tested all three leading models: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini
- All three misread the board positions — none confirmed checkmate

## What Each Model Did

### ChatGPT
- Verdict: "It does not look like a true checkmate"
- Claimed White had escape squares and blocking options
- Problem: Misidentified piece positions entirely

### Gemini
- Verdict: "Not a Checkmate, White's Turn"
- Described White King as unchallenged at top of board
- Problem: Described a game state that didn't match the image

### Claude
- Verdict: "I cannot confidently confirm this is checkmate from this image alone"
- Acknowledged pieces were hard to distinguish due to photo angle
- Could not pin down Black King's exact square
- Most transparent — chose honesty over confidence

## Key Observation
- All three models struggled with spatial interpretation from a physical photograph
- Angle, lighting, piece clustering from real-world photo throws off even the best models
- Text/code parsing vs. real-world image interpretation are fundamentally different problems
- Claude's approach (admitting uncertainty) more useful than false confidence

## Tone
- NOT a criticism of AI — they do incredible things
- Just a genuine, interesting observation — something revealing
- Appreciative of where AI is, curious about where it still surprises
